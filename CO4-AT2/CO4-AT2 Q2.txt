2.Manufacturing resource allocation(Dynamic programming)
A manufacturing company jas a limited production budget of Rs. 100,000 and serveral product options each product requires a certain investment and generates a specific profit. The company must determine the optimal combination to maximize total profit without exceeding the budget.
PRODUCT         INVESTMENT        PROFIT
A                20,000            25,000
B                30,000            40,000
C                40,000            50,000 
D                50,000            65,000
TASK:
1. Formula the problem as an optimization problem
2. Explain why Dynamic Programming is suitable for solving this problem
3. Develop the recurrence relation used in the solution 
4. Determine the solution product selection and maximum profit
5. Analyze the time and space complexity of the approach

Expected Concept : 01 knapsack problem,Dynamic programming optimal substructure

Answer:
1. Optimization Problem FormulationLet x_i in {0, 1} represent whether product i is selected (1) or not (0). To simplify, we scale the budget and investments by dividing by 10,000 (Budget W = 10).
Objective Function: Maximize profit Z:
Z = 25x_1 + 40x_2 + 50x_3 + 65x_4(in thousands)
Constraint:2x_1 + 3x_2 + 4x_3 + 5x_4 <= 10 (Budget Constraint)
2. Suitability of Dynamic Programming:
  Optimal Substructure: The optimal solution for a budget of Rs. 100,000 is built from optimal choices made for smaller sub-budgets.
  Overlapping Subproblems: Evaluating choices recursively causes redundant calculations of identical remaining budgets. DP avoids this by storing results in a table.
3. Recurrence RelationLet DP[i][w] be the max profit using up to item i with a scaled budget w :
DP[i][w] = { DP[i-1][w] & if  w_i > w 
max(DP[i-1][w], v_i + DP[i-1][w - w_i]) & if  w_i <= w 
}
4. Product Selection & Maximum ProfitBy filling out the 0/1 Knapsack decision matrix:Maximum Profit: Rs. 130,000 (Scaled value of 130)Optimal Product Selection: A, B, and D * Budget Verification:Rs.20,000 (A) + 30,000 (B) + 50,000 (D) = Rs.100,000 (Exactly hits the limit).
5. Complexity AnalysisTime Complexity: O(N x W) where N = 4 products and W = 10 budget steps. It takes a constant 44 matrix operations.
  Space Complexity: O(N x W) to store the table. This can be optimized to O(W) by storing only the previous array row.
